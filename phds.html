Luigi Chiricosta - Data Science

Reduce the biomedical features necessary for machine learning by exploiting the biological context

The biomedical sector has generated an immense amount of data over the past decade, and it shows no signs of diminishing.
On the contrary, it continues to grow steadily.
The declining costs associated with obtaining and analyzing biological samples from individuals to study complex diseases have made it possible to construct datasets large enough to leverage sophisticated mathematical techniques, including artificial intelligence, ranging from machine learning to deep learning.
The concept behind this PhD project involves developing a technique for feature selection or feature extraction that can reduce the amount of information required by machine learning models to make predictions, while considering the biological context. In addition to improving predictions and reducing the time required to build the model, this would also enable clinicians to understand the key biological characteristics that the model focuses on to distinguish the specific pathology.
Consequently, clinicians can then concentrate on determining the most effective treatment to adopt.

Simone D'Angiolini - Innovative techonologies in clinical medicine & dentistry

Analysis of big data through bioinformatics investigations for the identification of processes triggered by spinal cord injury and evaluation of possible treatments

It is necessary to clarify the mechanisms underlying secondary damage caused by spinal cord injury by analyzing the differences in transcriptomic profiles of the areas affected by primary damage and also exploring differences in the blood tissue transcriptome (already observed for other neurological diseases).
Testing the effectiveness of different treatments with mesenchymal stem cells and implants through studies on animal models and then evaluating their treatment on patients.
Potentially, the implemented project will provide useful information to determine the best adoptable therapy for spinal cord injury and how to evaluate its effectiveness.
